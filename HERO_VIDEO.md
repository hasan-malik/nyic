# #IChooseNY — Hero / 15-Second Concept Film

> The teaser that opens Slide 4 and lives on the campaign Instagram. Everything
> here is buildable on **Runway's free plan** (make a few accounts; each clip is
> short) + **ElevenLabs** credits + a free editor (CapCut / iMovie / Canva).
> Companion: [SLIDES.md](SLIDES.md), [PITCH.md](PITCH.md).

## The idea (one line)
**"Belonging isn't a transaction."** We never show paychecks or job titles — we show
*people choosing each other*, ending on the whole world arriving in New York and a
single human question. Warm, cinematic, golden-hour. Not an ad — a feeling.

## Specs
- **Two exports:** `9:16` 1080×1920 (Instagram/Reels, the real deliverable) and `16:9` 1920×1080 (embed in the deck). Shoot/generate 16:9, reframe vertical, or render both.
- **Length:** 15s teaser (below). A 60s "extended cut" is at the bottom.
- **Look:** golden hour, shallow depth of field, gentle handheld, film grain. Color: warm highlights, navy shadows (matches the brand).
- **Type:** Montserrat ExtraBold, white, lower-third; gold `#F0B429` for the hashtag.

## The 15-second storyboard (shot by shot)

|---|------|------|----------------|-------|
| 1 | 0.0–3.0s | **Central Park, golden hour.** Two friends sit on a bench, one holding a phone recording the other, both laughing. Slow push-in. | *(none — let it breathe)* | Soft piano starts. A faint real laugh. |
| 2 | 3.0–5.5s | **Middle-school classroom.** A student interviews a classmate with a phone on a little tripod; afternoon light. | **"A friend asks a friend:"** | Piano continues; light room tone. |

| 5 | 10.5–13.0s | **The globe.** Match-cut from the city to a dark, glowing Earth; gold arcs sweep from many countries and land on a bright NYC point. | **"120+ countries. One city."** | Single soft chime as arcs land. |

**Editing notes:** crossfade shots 1→4 (don't hard-cut; keep it tender). Shot 5 is the only "tech" moment — and you can screen-record it **straight from the live site** (`/constellation?view=origins`) instead of generating it, so it's unmistakably *your product*. Shot 4's pull-out is the signature "wow."

## Runway prompts (Gen-3 Alpha, image-to-video or text-to-video)
Generate each as a ~4s clip, then trim. Add `cinematic, golden hour, shallow depth of field, 35mm film grain, gentle handheld` to every prompt.

- **Shot 1:** `Two friends on a Central Park bench at golden hour, one filming the other on a phone, both laughing warmly, autumn leaves, slow push-in, bokeh background.`
- **Shot 2:** `A diverse middle-school classroom, one student interviewing a classmate with a phone on a small tripod, warm afternoon light through windows, documentary feel.`
- **Shot 3:** `An older immigrant woman and a young adult talking over tea in a cozy New York apartment kitchen, hands gesturing, intimate, warm window light.`
- **Shot 4:** `Aerial drone shot rising above Times Square at dusk, starting close on one couple recording a video and pulling up to reveal the full glowing crowd and billboards.`
- **Shot 5 (or screen-record the app instead):** `A dark stylized globe in deep navy space, glowing gold light-arcs traveling from many continents and converging on a bright point at New York City, elegant data-visualization style.`
- **Shot 6:** Make this in Canva/editor, not Runway — clean logo card on black.

> Free-plan tactic: generate 2–3 takes per shot, keep the best ~2.5–3s of each. You only need ~13s of footage total.

## ElevenLabs (voice + optional music)
1. **Voiceover** (Shot 3): one warm line — *"Why did you choose New York?"* Use a natural, gentle voice; lower stability ~40% for warmth. Export, place at 5.5s. **Optional:** layer two or three 1-word real-feeling answer whispers ("my mother…", "the subway…", "a stranger…") under Shot 4 for texture.
2. **Multilingual flex (great for judges):** render the same question in 2–3 languages (Spanish, Mandarin, Arabic) and quick-cut them under shots 2–3 — proves the multilingual promise in 2 seconds.
3. **Music:** a soft solo-piano-to-strings build, ~15s. Use a royalty-free bed (or ElevenLabs sound, or YouTube Audio Library "emotional piano"). Duck it -6dB under the VO.

---

## Assembly (CapCut/iMovie, ~30 min)
1. Drop shots 1–6 on the timeline in order; set durations per the table.
2. Add the music bed; fade in 0–1s, resolve at 14–15s.
3. Place VO at 5.5s; add the multilingual quick-cuts if using.
4. Add text overlays (table column 4); fade each in/out, keep on screen ≥1.5s.
5. Color: warm "teal-orange"-lite LUT; add subtle grain.
6. Export `9:16` 1080×1920 (IG) **and** `16:9` for the deck. Keep a local .mp4 for the talk.

## Extended 60-second cut (for the IG pin / longer reel)
Same spine, but let real interview audio carry it:
- **0–8s:** the three vignettes (park, classroom, kitchen) — no text, real laughter/room tone.
- **8–28s:** **a single real story excerpt** plays (Amina: *"I belong because someone chose me first."*) over slow b-roll of New York neighborhoods + a few seconds of the live **Boroughs/State-wide map** glowing.
- **28–45s:** montage of faces/places; quiet on-screen tags floating up (Belonging · Motherhood · Resilience · Faith) — literally your AI tags as motion text.
- **45–55s:** the globe arcs land on NYC; **"120+ countries. One city. 3,700+ voices and counting."**
- **55–60s:** logo card + CTA: **"Record a friend. #IChooseNY"** → `ichooseny.netlify.app`.
